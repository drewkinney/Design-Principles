# Visceral / Alignment — Aesthetic Effect

Aesthetic alignment: does the interface prime the correct emotional expectation before any action?
Aesthetics built in = primed expectation. Aesthetics applied after = decoration.
Attractive interfaces test as more usable before use begins — the perception is neurologically real.

Score: PASSES | FAILS | MIXED | UNSCORED

---

## Evaluate

- Does the first screen produce trust or neutrality?
- Does the visual treatment feel considered or assembled?
- Is there evidence of aesthetic intent in typography, spacing, and image choice?
- Does the aesthetic match the lead emotion identified in PRE-AUDIT.md?
- Does the interface make a commitment — or hedge toward safe?

Failure modes: generic component libraries applied without visual judgment, inconsistent
type scales, stock photography signaling no effort spent, aesthetic contradicting the
intended emotional target, cautious choices that produce neutral rather than trust.

PASSES: first screen produces trust; aesthetic matches the lead emotion.
MIXED: trust present but aesthetic misaligns with emotional target, or hedges safe.
FAILS: first screen produces neutral or negative response; no discernible aesthetic intent.
