# Reflective / Momentum — Memory Residue

Behavioral momentum either leaves residue or it doesn't.
Smooth interactions are invisible in memory — users remember the outcome, not the path.
Friction-laden interactions are remembered as effort, regardless of whether they succeeded.
Residue is what determines whether users return willingly.

Score: PASSES | FAILS | MIXED | UNSCORED

---

## Evaluate

- After completing the primary task, what would users remember as difficult?
- Are there interaction moments likely to be recalled as frustrating or confusing?
- Does the interface end interactions cleanly — or leave tasks feeling unresolved?
- Are confirmation and success states satisfying, or bureaucratic?
- Is perceived effort proportional to the value received?

Failure modes: confirmation states that feel like form submissions rather than completed
goals, interactions requiring multiple attempts before succeeding, flows that leave users
uncertain whether they finished what they intended, closing states that don't acknowledge
the task was done.

PASSES: no interaction moments likely to be remembered as friction; outcomes feel resolved.
MIXED: most interactions clean; one or two moments likely to be recalled as friction.
FAILS: interaction friction is memorable; effort likely to outweigh perceived value.
